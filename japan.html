Notice of Material Confiscation

Form CCD-7  /  Revised October 1946  /  GHQ Tokyo
Pursuant to SCAPIN-33 (Press Code for Japan, September 19, 1945) and SCAPIN-16 (Removal of Restrictions on Freedom of Thought), the material requested by the above-named party has been identified as containing content in violation of Allied Occupation directives and has been confiscated and placed under restricted access by order of the Civil Censorship Detachment, GHQ/SCAP.

Applicable Directives
Article 2(d) — Material that may disturb public tranquility.
Article 4(a) — Criticism, direct or implied, of Allied Forces or SCAP.
Article 4(f) — References to atomic bomb casualties or effects not approved by CCD.
Article 6(b) — Content deemed prejudicial to the objectives of the Allied Occupation.
Article 9 — Material originating from or relating to suppressed organizations.
Right of Appeal
The affected party may submit a formal appeal to the Civil Censorship Detachment, Attention: Review Board, GHQ Building 8, Tokyo. Appeals must be submitted in writing within 14 calendar days of this notice. Processing time: 180 business days. The CCD's decision on appeal is final and not subject to further review. Submission of an appeal does not guarantee return of confiscated materials.
CONFISCATED
This notice is issued under the authority of General Douglas MacArthur, Supreme Commander for the Allied Powers. Interference with confiscation proceedings constitutes a violation of Occupation law and may result in arrest by Military Police. All confiscated materials become property of GHQ/SCAP and will not be returned unless ordered by the Review Board. This document must be retained by the recipient for a period of not less than three years.
